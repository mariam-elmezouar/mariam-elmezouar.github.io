General Information

The course covers a range of topics integral to the design, implementation, and testing of a medium-scale software system with the practical experience of implementing such a project as a member of a programmer team. The course also includes material on the social context of computing as well as professionalism and ethical responsibilities in software development.

At the end of this course, the student should understand the complexity of software development projects and the ongoing lifecycle. In particular, they will be able to apply multiple design and implementation methodologies with a sound understanding of quality control and assurance through testing and defect resolution. They will have been exposed to several real world use cases exposing the challenges and impacts of decisions in both the development process and software lifecycle; including the consideration of social and ethical impacts.

CASE Tools

CASE stands for Computer Aided Software Engineering. CASE tools are set of software application programs, which are used to automate SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) activities. CASE tools are used by software project managers, analysts and engineers to develop software system.

In this course, we will be using the CASE tools from the Jira platform, to manage the software development activities performed in the context of the group project.

Academic Integrity

Overview

The aim of this part of your coursework is to give you a chance to try out some of the things you will hear in lecture and to experience what it is like to work on a software project over a short time frame.

The six of you will work as a team. Each team member will need to write code, and will need to adopt other roles as needed.

The purpose of your first team meeting in week 2 will be to make a start on choosing a project theme, and the roles of primary software architect and team manager.

You will be invited to join a set of CASE tools based on JIRA. Jira is linked to document creation tools, a git repository and a chat system. Either Slack or MS Teams will be used as a chat tool. You must use the CASE tools supplied. Your project work, deliverables and communications must be fully documented. You are strongly discouraged from using social media tools like Facebook for project work.

The rules

This list is not necessarily complete. Just as in industry - why should you know all the rules imposed by Management ahead of time? It is often Management's role to seemingly impede your project by changing the goals while the work is in progress!

  • Your project must be sufficiently large and challenging, but not impossible. Management reserves the right to further refine your project.
  • The bulk of your project should be coded in a language of your choice, pre-approved by management. You may use whatever compilers and libraries you like.
  • Your software must have a GUI or web-browser interface. It can run on a PC, a Mac or a portable device (or all of the above). You won't be able to sell anything that just has a text-only interface.
  • Every decision your team makes, every meeting you hold, every bug you fix, etc. MUST be documented in Jira by using applicable issues. You should not be working on anything that is not first documented as an issue, a "task", which is part of a sprint. Your team will expect you to estimate a time to completion and document your progress. Documents and all code must be available to each team member as well as to Upper Management (the prof). Meeting minutes must list participants. Documentation is a large annoyance - but a necessary part of software engineering and legal accountability.
  • You must use your CASE tools to drive your project efforts. Create issues before you do the work, not after the fact to satisfy your grader! You must track progress and issue completion, for example. You will not be able to show burn-down charts if you do not track progress. Build sprints, add comments to your commits, and so forth. No planning or work should be done without your CASE tools being aware of it. Our tools are designed to work together. For example you can create links between documents in Confluence and issues in Jira for ease of navigation.
