Tasks

Task 1:

Architects Limited, a large firm employing about 500 people, has decided to develop a database to effectively manage its rapid growth. This database will maintain records of employees, departmental details, and project information.

Each employee is assigned a unique number upon joining the company. The database records each employee's name, address, and job start date. Employees are categorized into roles such as architects, designers, or administrators, with architects having specific hourly rates, designers identified by design types, and administrators distinguished by their specialties. Additionally, some employees do not fit into these categories.

The company is composed of twelve distinct departments, each with a unique name and its own telephone and fax numbers. Although a department may have multiple employees, each employee is affiliated with only one department. Departments are headed by supervisors, who manage one department at a time.

When the firm accepts a new project, typically a group of employees is assigned to it. There is a mix of architects, designers and administrators, selected based on the alignment of individual experience with project needs. Each project is uniquely identified and accompanied by a detailed description, including drawings, start date, and budget. Employees may work on multiple projects, often in varying roles (e.g., project manager in one project and designer in another).

The experience or specialties are identified by a code. For each of the specialties, there is a brief description. Typically, an employee has more than one specialty, and several employees share the same specialty. Usually, a project requires several specialties and the same specialty can be used by several projects. For each of the specialties required by a project, the firm will indicate the number of employees required in this specialty for the project in question.

Draw an ER diagram describing this situation. Make sure that all types of entities have at least one attribute that will serve as a key. Clearly state any assumptions you make when developing your diagram. You can use one of the following editors.When done, make sure you export the drawing in an appropriate format (PDF)

  • draw.io: A free online diagram software ideal for creating ER diagrams. It integrates with platforms like Google Drive and Dropbox and offers a wide range of shapes and templates.
  • Lucidchart: Offers a free version for drawing ER diagrams with an intuitive interface and collaborative features. Advanced features may require a subscription.
  • Dia: A free and open-source diagramming tool, suitable for creating ER diagrams. It's more technical and offers extensive features for detailed diagrams.
  • yEd Graph Editor: A free tool for creating ER diagrams, known for its range of layout algorithms to arrange complex diagrams.
  • StarUML: A sophisticated software modeling tool with ER diagram support. The basic version is free and suitable for complex projects.

Task 2:

Based on the information provided in Task 1, create an Enhanced Entity-Relationship (EER) diagram that includes advanced concepts such as specialization, generalization, inheritance, and any participation / disjointness constraints. Consider aspects such as the various roles of employees (architects, designers, administrators), the hierarchical structure within the company, and the nature of project assignments. Ensure that your diagram accurately reflects these relationships and hierarchies within Architects Limited. Export your EER diagram in an appropriate format (PDF) and include any assumptions or specific details you used in your representation.
